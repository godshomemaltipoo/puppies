The Maltipoo Breed

The Maltipoo is a popular crossbreed between the Maltese and the Toy or Miniature Poodle. Known for their affectionate, fun-loving nature, they make wonderful family companions. They are intelligent, easy to train, and have a low-shedding, hypoallergenic coat, making them a great choice for people with allergies.

  • Size: Typically 5-20 lbs
  • Lifespan: 10-15 years
  • Temperament: Gentle, playful, intelligent
  • Grooming: Regular brushing & professional grooming

Our Adoption Process

We've streamlined our process to be simple, transparent, and focused on the well-being of our puppies.

1. Inquiry & Application

Start by reaching out to us with any questions. We'll guide you through our application to ensure our puppies go to the best homes. A deposit will reserve your chosen companion.

2. Weekly Updates

Once you've reserved your puppy, you'll receive weekly photos and videos. Watch your new family member grow and develop their personality from afar.

3. Gotcha Day!

When your puppy is ready (around 8-10 weeks), we'll arrange for their safe journey to you, complete with a health certificate, vet records, and a starter pack.

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our puppies and process.

What comes with my puppy?

Each puppy comes with their first set of vaccinations, a deworming record, a thorough vet check-up, a health guarantee, a small bag of their current food, and a blanket with their mother's scent.

Do you require a deposit?

Yes, we require a non-refundable deposit to reserve a puppy. This deposit is applied toward the final price of your puppy. It ensures serious inquiries and secures your place in choosing a puppy.

How are the puppies socialized?

Our puppies are raised in our home as part of our family. They are handled daily and exposed to common household sounds, children, and other pets to ensure they are well-adjusted and confident.

What is the typical size of your Maltipoos?

Our Maltipoos typically range from 7 to 14 pounds as adults, depending on the parents. We can give you a more accurate estimate based on the specific litter you are interested in.
